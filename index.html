About

Profile Summary

I have earned Bachelor's, Master's, and Ph.D. degrees from Dalian University of Technology. In 2016-2017, I embarked on a prestigious scholarship-funded academic journey to the University of British Columbia, Canada. My primary research expertise encompasses Big Data Computation and Multimodal Data Fusion.

Academic Contributions

I have made significant contributions to the academic world with over 50 publications in renowned national and international journals and conferences. Notably, I have authored 30 of these publications as the first author or corresponding author. My research has resulted in the successful granting of 15 national invention patents and the registration of 2 software copyrights. Research Focus

(1) Conducting research on models for fusing low-quality and heterogeneous multimodal data, addressing issues such as mismatch, incompleteness, inaccuracy, and imbalance.

(2) Analyzing medical and industrial data based on multimodal fusion theories.

Research

multimodal learning

Data fusion method is an important means of multi-modal data analysis and mining. However, the modal incompleteness, high-dimensionality and mismatch of multi-modal data pose severe challenges to the design of fusion and clustering methods. This group is committed to studying multi-modal clustering problems under the above circumstances, and strives for more robust representation learning and clustering methods. The main research directions are multi-modal alignment and incomplete multi-modal clustering, which are used to solve the problems of sample misalignment and missing samples in reality.

Cross-modal generation

Our work focuses on the field of image generation and processing, and the functions implemented include: image generation based on text description, image editing and image repair, etc. The main knowledge involved in the work includes advanced technologies such as GAN networks, CNN networks, diffusion models, autoencoders, and autoregressive models to achieve innovative and efficient image generation and processing technologies.

medical group

Our team uses multi-modal medical images and medical relational data to perform tasks such as disease determination, lesion segmentation, and condition classification.Specific directions include: multi-modal medical segmentation network based on hybrid attention mechanism, incomplete modal brain tumor image segmentation based on statistical features and correlation fusion, brain tumor image segmentation based on self-supervised multi-modal fusion network, Vision Transformer-based lung cancer time series data classification network, sepsis condition prediction based on ensemble learning, etc. It aims to mine potential features from medical data that can assist doctors in disease diagnosis and treatment, highlight pathological characteristics of diseases, or provide auxiliary diagnosis results.
